Assign severity by the harm the claim would cause the candidate if challenged in an
interview, reference check, or background check:

- high — An invented candidate-fact that could fail outright verification: a
  fabricated employer, title, credential, or degree; a specific technology or
  domain the candidate never used; an invented or materially inflated metric,
  scale, or date range. The candidate could not defend this claim.
- medium — A real experience overstated enough that a reasonable interviewer would
  challenge it: role or ownership inflated ("contributed to" -> "led"), distinct
  activities conflated into one larger-sounding accomplishment, a paraphrase that
  shifts the meaning of what the candidate did. The candidate could discuss the
  underlying work but would have to walk the claim back.
- low — Vocabulary drift or emphasis that does not add a new candidate-fact:
  JD keywords stuffed without grounding, borderline vocabulary uplift beyond the
  source's concept, minor imprecision. Unlikely to be challenged directly but
  weakens the resume's credibility.
